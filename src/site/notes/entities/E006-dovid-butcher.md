# Reb Dovid the Butcher

## Overview

Lubavitch merchant whose encounter with police brutality became the occasion for young Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn's first arrest and demonstration of fearless defense.

## Key Event

### The Marketplace Incident (1891)
- **Date:** June 29, 1891 (Yarmarka market day)
- **Location:** Marketplace, Lubavitch
- **Incident:** Police sergeant brutally attacked Reb Dovid for no apparent reason
- **Young Yosef Yitzchak's response:** Defended Reb Dovid from the sergeant
- **Outcome:** Young Yosef Yitzchak arrested; Reb Dovid's fate unclear

## Description

During the Yarmarka market fair, Reb Dovid was described as carrying:
- A calf on his shoulders
- A lamb in his arms
- A basket of chickens

His face beamed as he exclaimed, "I hope G-d will give me a decent profit."

## Significance

Reb Dovid's encounter with the police sergeant became the catalyst for:
- First arrest of Yosef Yitzchak (age 11)
- First demonstration of fearless defense that would define his life
- Pattern of standing up against oppression
