# R. Aronovski

The Rav of Velizh who served as an intermediary during the conflict regarding the proposed Leningrad Congregation meeting in 1927.

---

## Role in Events

In the days leading up to his arrest (Sunday-Tuesday before 15 Sivan 1927), the Rebbe Rayatz engaged in verbal exchanges regarding a controversial proposal by the Leningrad Congregation to hold a general meeting.

The communication was conducted via:
- **R. Aronovski**, Rav of Velizh
- The Rav of Sdeh Menuchah
- **R. David Tevel Katznelenbogen**, the elderly rabbi involved

---

## The Controversy

The Leningrad Congregation wanted to hold a general meeting. The Rebbe Rayatz opposed this meeting, believing it was:
- A conspiracy "to smite our country's Jews in the apple of their eye"
- An attack on Yiddishkeit in the Soviet Union

R. Katznelenbogen, however, indicated his intention to oppose the Rebbe's stand — not based on merits, but explicitly because the Rebbe was against it.

The Rebbe described this period as "most distressful days" that affected "the whole state of Yiddishkeit in our country."

---

## Outcome

Thankfully, "for various reasons" the proposal was never realized. The Rebbe expressed gratitude: "Praise G-d for His kindness, for having arranged the circumstances that made that proposal fall away."
