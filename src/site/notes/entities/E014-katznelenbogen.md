# R. David Tevel Katznelenbogen

An elderly rabbi involved in the controversy over the proposed Leningrad Congregation meeting in 1927.

---

## The Controversy (1927)

Before the Rebbe Rayatz's arrest in June 1927, there was a conflict regarding a proposed general meeting of the Leningrad Congregation.

### Positions
- **The Rebbe Rayatz**: Opposed the meeting, believing it was a conspiracy against Jewish life
- **R. Katznelenbogen**: Supported the meeting, explicitly stating he intended to oppose the Rebbe's stand

### Communication
Exchanges were conducted via intermediaries:
- R. Aronovski (Rav of Velizh)
- The Rav of Sdeh Menuchah

### The Rebbe's Account

> "R. Katznelenbogen did not realize that the members of that congregation were acting deviously. Moreover, as he told one of the above rabbis explicitly, his intention was to oppose my stand: since I held that this meeting should not be held, he was not interested in discussing the rights and wrongs of the actual question, but felt himself obliged to side with the opposite party."

---

## Outcome

The proposal "was never realized" — for which the Rebbe expressed gratitude, calling it a deliverance from "a conspiracy to smite our country's Jews in the apple of their eye."
