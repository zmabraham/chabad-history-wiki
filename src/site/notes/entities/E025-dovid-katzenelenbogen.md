# Rabbi Dovid Tevel Katzenelenbogen

Chief Rabbi of Leningrad during the 1920s.

## Position

- **Chief Rabbi of Leningrad**
- Led the Jewish community during the period of intense Soviet persecution

## Relationship with the Rebbe

The Rebbe was in communication with Rabbi Katzenelenbogen and found himself in **"deep distress"** because of their discussions.

### The "General Assembly" Dispute

Anti-religious Jews had scheduled a "General Assembly" of Leningrad's communal leaders. They had:
- **Deceived the Chief Rabbi** into siding with them
- Created **false issues of personal conflict** to undermine traditional Judaism

The Rebbe **opposed this assembly**, recognizing it as a scheme to undermine traditional Jewish leadership. This opposition came on the very night of his arrest (14 Sivan 5687 / June 14, 1927).

## Historical Context

During this period:
- The Yevsektzia was actively working to destroy Jewish religious life
- Traditional rabbis were under constant threat of arrest
- Any Jewish leader who cooperated with anti-religious forces was seen as betraying the community
