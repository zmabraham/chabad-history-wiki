# Kostroma

The city to which the Frierdiker Rebbe was sentenced to three years of exile in 1927, after the capital charge was quashed.

---

## The Exile Sentence

On **3 Tammuz 5687 (1927)**, after 18 days of imprisonment, the capital charge was quashed and commuted to:
- **Three years of exile** in Kostroma
- Located on the Volga River
- A remote city, far from Jewish communities

### Timeline to Kostroma

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|

---

## The Farewell Message

Before boarding the train to exile on 3 Tammuz, the Rebbe stood on the train step and delivered his famous farewell message to the chassidim who had accompanied him to the station:

> "We must always bear in mind that prisons and hard-labor camps are transient, whereas the Torah, its mitzvos, and the Jewish people, are eternal."

---

## Liberation

The exile lasted less than two weeks. On **Yud-Beis Tammuz (12 Tammuz)**, the Rebbe was informed of his impending release. On **Yud-Gimmel Tammuz (13 Tammuz)** at 12:30 PM, he was liberated — a day celebrated as **Yud-Beis Tammuz** in Chabad.
