# Nachmanson

A Jewish GPU officer from Nevel who participated in the arrest of the Frierdiker Rebbe on 15 Sivan 1927.

---

## Background

- Born in Nevel to a family that had connections to Lubavitch
- His father used to visit Lubavitch
- Educated in the school system in Nevel
- Became an officer in the GPU (Soviet secret police)

---

## Role in the Arrest

During the midnight arrest on 15 Sivan 1927, Nachmanson was the spokesman for the GPU unit. After the Rebbe Rayatz gave his "cool and unimpressed response" — or as the Rebbe called it, "the naive scornfulness of a Lubavitcher citizen" — Nachmanson was the first to speak.

He ordered the armed guards:
> "Go out and stand guard at the doors. If anyone wants to enter, open wide. If people want to move about, even from room to room, or even to talk with each other, don't let them! You've been warned!"

---

## Significance

Nachmanson represents a tragic element of the period — Jews from traditional backgrounds who became enforcers of Soviet policy against their own communities. His familiarity with Lubavitch (through his father) makes his role in the arrest particularly poignant.
