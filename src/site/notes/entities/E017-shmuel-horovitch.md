# R. Shmuel Horovitch

A chassid in whose home the Rashab stayed when visiting Mozinkes; a former "sitter" (yoshiv) in Lubavitch during the time of the Tzemach Tzedek.

---

## Background

- Born in **Deneburg** (Daugavpils, Latvia)
- As a young man, was one of the advanced long-term students (**yoshvim**) in Lubavitch
- Studied in Lubavitch from **5608-5611 (1848-1851)** during the leadership of the **Tzemach Tzedek**

---

## Scholarship

Described as:
- "A solid scholar who studied Gemara and the poskim in depth"
- Had memorized **three Sedarim of Mishnayos** as directed by the Tzemach Tzedek at yechidus
- Had mastered **Torah Or** and **Likkutei Torah** ( foundational Chassidus texts by the Tzemach Tzedek)
- A man of upright character

---

## Life Path

### Early Years
A learned villager took him as a son-in-law and supported him for several years of full-time study.

### Farming
After marriage, R. Shmuel worked as a farmer in his village. When the timber business reached the region, he converted buildings in his backyard into accommodations for lumber merchants.

### Mozinkes
The Rashab stayed in R. Shmuel's home when visiting Mozinkes for health reasons, at the insistence of Dr. Bogorodski.

---

## The Yoshvim of Lubavitch

R. Shmuel shared memories of the two groups of students in the Tzemach Tzedek's time:

| Group | Purpose | Age Range | Study Focus |
|-------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Yeshivah students** | Traditional yeshivah | 14-20 | Gemara, poskim; occasional Tanya/maamarim |
| **Yoshvim** (sitters) | Chassidic immersion | Varies | Entire occupation with Chassidus; serving elder chassidim |

### The Yoshvim comprised:
1. Young men (before or after marriage)
2. Gifted young students
3. Men whose fathers supported them
4. Those who weren't outstanding scholars but absorbed the "ambiance of Lubavitch"

---

## Philosophy of Service

R. Shmuel emphasized the importance of being in the company of elder chassidim:

> "In the days of the Tzemach Tzedek, at almost all times through the year there were venerable and hoary chassidim visiting Lubavitch. So there were always opportunities to be of service to great men, to observe meanwhile how they davened and how they ate, how they studied and how they conducted themselves."

> "Sometimes this affected young scholars even more than their actual studies."

Citing the Talmud: "Being of service to Torah sages is superior [even] to the study of the Torah itself" (Berachos 7b)
