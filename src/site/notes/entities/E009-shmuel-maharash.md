# Rabbi Shmuel (Maharash)

## Overview

Fourth Lubavitcher Rebbe (1834-1882), father of the Rashab. Known for his famous saying "The world is a narrow bridge; the main thing is not to fear at all."

## Key Facts

- **Born:** 1834
- **Died:** Shortly after Yom Kippur 1882
- **Age at death:** 48
- **Leadership:** 16 years as fourth Rebbe
- **Father:** Rabbi Menachem Mendel (Tzemach Tzedek)
- **Son:** Rabbi Shalom Dovber (Rashab)

## Leadership

### Becoming Rebbe (1866)
- Succeeded his father, Rabbi Menachem Mendel (Tzemach Tzedek)
- Was the youngest son
- Took leadership despite having older brothers

### Passing (1882)
- **Date:** Shortly after Yom Kippur 1882
- **Last words to wife:** "You will rise up thirty-two steps in a physical dimension and I will rise up thirty-two steps in a spiritual way"
- **Age:** 48
- **Leadership duration:** 16 years

## The Leadership Crisis

After Rabbi Shmuel's passing, a leadership crisis emerged:

### The Meeting
- **Location:** Home of Rebbetzin Rivkah (his widow)
- **Date:** 8 Cheshvan 5643 (October 21, 1882)
- **Expected successors:** Two older sons
  - Rabbi Zalman Aharon (Raza), age 24
  - Rabbi Shalom Dovber (Rashab), almost 22

### The Outcome
- Both sons initially refused leadership
- Created 11-year period before Rashab fully accepted
- Rashab formally became Rebbe on Rosh HaShanah 1893

## Simchat Torah 1879

### The Blessing Oversight
During Simchat Torah 1879, Rabbi Shmuel blessed each family member but overlooked his daughter-in-law, Rebbetzin Shterna Sara. This incident led to:

1. Her tears and distress
2. Her prophetic dream of three Rebbes promising her a son
3. Birth of Yosef Yitzchak the following year (1880)

## Famous Sayings

> "The world is a narrow bridge; the main thing is not to fear at all."

This saying, attributed to the Maharash, became famous in Jewish music and thought.

## Significance

- Fourth in the line of Lubavitcher Rebbes
- His death created leadership crisis that shaped succession patterns
- His oversight of blessing led to prophetic dream about Rayatz's birth
- His "thirty-two steps" statement had prophetic significance
