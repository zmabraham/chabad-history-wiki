# Lulav (G.P.U. Official)

Soviet G.P.U. (secret police) official of Jewish origin from the Lulav family of Riga, Latvia. Active in Leningrad during the 1920s.

## Role in 1927 Arrest

Lulav was **Nachmanson's** associate during the arrest and imprisonment of the Frierdiker Rebbe, Rabbi Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn, on **14 Sivan 5687 (June 14, 1927)**.

**Key Actions:**
- Present at the Rebbe's home during the arrest
- Accompanied Nachmanson during the search of the Rebbe's residence
- Described as "short dark-haired Jewish man from the family of that name in Riga"
- Nachmanson instructed Lulav to "begin their work" when searching the apartment

## Post-Arrest Activities

After the Rebbe's liberation on **12 Tammuz 5687 (July 12, 1927)**, Lulav joined with Nachmanson and **Messing** in mounting a sustained campaign against the Rebbe:
- Published harsh articles in *Der Emes* (the Yevsektzia newspaper)
- Demanded the Rebbe's arrest and imprisonment in Siberia
- Depicted the Rebbe as a "counter-enlightenment personality" beyond the law
- Their campaign contributed to the Rebbe's eventual decision to leave Russia

## Historical Context

Lulav was part of the **Yevsektzia** (Jewish Sections of the Communist Party) — Jewish Communists who fought particularly fiercely against religious Judaism, believing they needed to be "more ruthless with rabbis than non-Jewish Communists."

## Family Background

The Lulav family was a known Jewish family in **Riga, Latvia**. The fact that Lulav is identified by his family name suggests either:
1. The family was prominent enough to be recognizable
2. His actions were notorious enough that his identification became known
