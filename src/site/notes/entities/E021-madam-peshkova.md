# Madam Peshkova

Prominent Soviet activist who headed the **Political Prisoner Relief** organization and played a crucial role in securing the liberation of the Frierdiker Rebbe in 1927.

## Role in the Rebbe's Liberation

### Initial Efforts

After the Rebbe's arrest on **14 Sivan 5687 (June 14, 1927)**:
- International pressure mounted for his release
- Madam Peshkova was asked to intervene on behalf of the imprisoned Rebbe
- She was already known for her work helping political prisoners in Soviet Russia

### During Kostroma Exile

When the Rebbe was exiled to **Kostroma** for three years:
- **Moscow group** of chassidim and supporters worked for complete liberation
- They approached **Messing** (high Soviet official) requesting the Rebbe's return to Leningrad
- Messing obstinately refused, warning the Rebbe would face new pretexts for re-imprisonment
- Madam Peshkova's emissary returned with this negative response

### Final Success

The Moscow group was undeterred:
- Decided to persist until achieving **complete, unconditional release**
- Madam Peshkova made **"intensive endeavors"** to exert influence
- Finally succeeded in reaching **"the highest rungs of Soviet officialdom"**
- On **12 Tammuz (July 12)**, the order came from Moscow granting full freedom

## The Political Prisoner Relief Organization

Madam Peshkova's organization:
- Advocated for political prisoners in Soviet Russia
- Had enough standing to approach high Soviet officials
- Maintained international connections
- Successfully navigated Soviet bureaucracy to achieve prisoner releases

## Historical Significance

Madam Peshkova represents:
1. **Soviet citizens who used their position** to help victims of persecution
2. **International pressure's effectiveness** when channeled through proper channels
3. **The complexity of Soviet politics** — not all officials were monolithic in opposition to religious figures
4. **Women's role** in humanitarian efforts during this period
