# Rabbi Eliyahu Chaim Althaus (Reb Eliyahu Chaim Althaus)

Close chassid of the Frierdiker Rebbe who served as his secretary and accompanied him during his 1927 exile to Kostroma. His detailed letters provide important eyewitness testimony.

## Role During 1927 Arrest and Exile

### At the Arrest

When G.P.U. agents raided the Rebbe's home on **14 Sivan 5687 (June 14, 1927)**:
- Reb Althaus was present during the confrontation
- Witnessed the exchange between the Rebbe and G.P.U. officials
- Would later document these events in his memoirs

### Journey to Kostroma

After 19 days in Spalerno prison, the Rebbe was exiled to Kostroma:
- **Reb Althaus accompanied the Rebbe** on the train journey
- Also traveling: the Rebbe's future son-in-law **[[mmm]]**, and the Rebbe's daughter, Rebbetzin **Chaya Moussia**
- The group was under **heavy guard**: Cheka secret police, civil police, soldiers, and investigation officers

### At the Liberation

On **12 Tammuz (July 12, 1927)**, the day of liberation:
- Reb Althaus accompanied the Rebbe to the weekly G.P.U. appearance
- Present when the official announced: *"You are totally freed... The order has been received to grant you full freedom"*
- His reaction was so intense that *"his face went from deep vivid to palest white and back"*
- The Rebbe had to **calm him and help him regain his composure**

## His Letters — Primary Historical Source

Reb Eliyahu Chaim Althaus wrote detailed letters documenting:
- Events before and during the arrest
- The prison experience
- The liberation
- The Rebbe's words and actions throughout

These letters are preserved as:
- **Appendix 7** in *The Heroic Struggle*
- Titled: *"A Chassid's Memoirs: From Two Letters of Reb Eliyahu Chaim Althaus"*
- Span pages 255-314 in the original edition

## Value as Historical Testimony

His accounts are particularly valuable because:
1. **Eyewitness perspective** — present at key moments
2. **Emotional authenticity** — his genuine reactions recorded
3. **Attention to detail** — documented conversations and events
4. **Chassidic viewpoint** — understood spiritual significance
5. **Immediate recording** — written while memories were fresh

## Historical Context

Reb Althaus was one of the **"nine dedicated temimim"** who in **Adar 5684 (1924)** made a binding covenant with the Rebbe to work to strengthen Judaism **"even to the last drop of blood."**
