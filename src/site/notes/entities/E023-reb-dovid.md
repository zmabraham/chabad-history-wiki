# Reb Dovid (the Butcher)

Lubavitch merchant known for his piety and optimism. A key figure in young Yosef Yitzchak's childhood, Reb Dovid was one of the first beneficiaries of the future Rebbe's chesed (humanitarian assistance) loan fund.

## Background

- **Location**: Lubavitch
- **Occupation**: Butcher and livestock merchant
- **Character**: Known for his beaming face and faith in G-d

## The Loan Fund

In the summer of 1891, young Yosef Yitzchak (age 11) had saved over 30 rubles from studying Mishnah. Following his father's suggestion to use the money for chesed, he began making microloans to fellow Jews.

Reb Dovid was among the first recipients. Yosef Yitzchak would later recount:

> *"He was carrying a calf on his shoulders, a lamb in his arms, and a basket of chickens hanging in front of him. His face beamed as he exclaimed, 'I hope G-d will give me a decent profit.'"*

## Relationship with Young Yosef Yitzchak

Reb Dovid was well acquainted with Yosef Yitzchak, who had been staking him with microloans from his fund so that Dovid could purchase livestock from nearby farms and resell it in town.
