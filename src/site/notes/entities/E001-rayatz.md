# Rabbi Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn (The Rayatz)

## Overview

Sixth Lubavitcher Rebbe (1880-1950), one of the most consequential figures of the modern Jewish era. Stood at the apex of history through czarist oppression, communist persecution, European upheaval, and American assimilation.

## Key Facts

- **Born:** June 30, 1880 (12 Tamuz 5640), Lubavitch, White Russia
- **Died:** 1950
- **Father:** \[\[Rabbi Shalom Dovber Schneersohn (The Rashab)\]\]
- **Became Rebbe:** 1920
- **Wife:** Rebbetzin Nechama Dina
- **Succeeded by:** Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson (The Ramash)

## Leadership Challenges Faced

1. **Czarist Russia** — Early years facing oppression
2. **Communist Soviet Union** — Rise of communism seeking to eradicate Jewish traditions
3. **Westernized Europe** — Adapting Chabad Chasidism outside Eastern Europe
4. **America** — Reimagining Jewish life amid assimilation

## Core Characteristics

- **Undaunted courage** — Bucked conventional wisdom repeatedly
- **Adaptability** — Successfully started anew in different countries
- **Spiritual rootedness** — First and foremost rooted in deep spiritual values
- **Torah scholar** — Left 200+ volumes of maamarim, talks, and writings
- **Activist** — Organizer and leader despite health issues and limited finances
- **Compassion** — Boundless compassion for Jews everywhere
- **Sense of mission** — Instilled by his father, the Rashab

## Famous Quote

> "Without a yeshivah there is no life."

## Legacy

- Created path for bright Jewish future amidst unparalleled disruption
- Reimagined Jewish life in America, laying foundation for renaissance of tradition
- Through thousands of letters, addresses, and accounts, his voice continues to resonate
