# R. Yechezkel

The Rav of Kharkov, described as dignified with his gold spectacles.

---

## Description

The young Rayatz first encountered R. Yechezkel during the family's stay in Kharkov (Lag BaOmer 5646 / 1886). He was particularly impressed by:

> "The local rav, R. Yechezkel, who looked so dignified with his gold spectacles."

---

## The Kharkov Farbrengen

R. Yechezkel was present at the memorable farbrengen where:
- Many grown men, including elderly chassidim with white beards, crowded into a middle-sized room
- All attention focused on the Rashab at the head of a long narrow table
- When the Rashab spoke, complete silence fell over the room
- Some listeners shed tears during the discourse

### During the Farbrengen
After the discourse, when celebrations began and the young Rayatz was passed from chassid to chassid, R. Yechezkel:
- Offered the young boy a cup of mashke to say LeChaim
- The boy declined, citing the proper order of blessings (Mezonos before Shehakol)
- R. Yechezkel smiled and provided cake first
- A "flicker of a smile" was seen on the faces of the Rashab and others

---

## Character

Appears as a warm and understanding figure who:
- Participated joyously in the farbrengen
- Was patient with the young boy's halachic precision
- Smiled at the child's proper observance of blessing order
