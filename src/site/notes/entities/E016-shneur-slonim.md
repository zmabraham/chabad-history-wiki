# R. Shneur Slonim

The teacher (melamed) of the young Yosef Yitzchak during family stays in Yalta.

---

## Role

Served as the personal tutor to the young Rayatz during the family's time in Yalta, Crimea. Taught him for approximately one hour each day during their walks.

---

## Teaching Relationship

The young Rayatz was initially resistant to having R. Shneur as a teacher:

> "At that time I persuaded my mother — in exchange for a promise to take my studies more seriously, because this was when R. Shneur had first arrived and I was not at all eager to have him as my teacher — to tell me the reason for her distress."

The promise to study energetically with R. Shneur became a turning point in the young boy's commitment to learning.

---

## In Yalta

During the stays in Yalta, the daily routine was:
- The family would walk from 1:00 PM until 7:00 or 8:00 PM
- The Rashab would find a seat and study
- R. Shneur would teach young Yosef Yitzchak for about an hour
- R. Shneur would then join the Rashab, and they would study together
- The Rebbetzin would sit nearby, reading letters and doing needlework

The Rebbe describes their study sessions:
> "My father would speak and R. Shneur would listen; R. Shneur would pose a problematic query and my father would offer a solution; my father would clarify a point and R. Shneur's face would glow."

---

## Relationship with the Rashab

R. Shneur had a close learning relationship with the Rashab (Rabbi Shalom Dovber Schneersohn). They would study "a learned book" together during the Yalta stays, with R. Shneur asking questions and receiving clarifications that visibly moved him.
