# Spiritual Courage: The Rebbe's Response to Persecution

The Frierdiker Rebbe's arrest and imprisonment in 1927 demonstrated extraordinary spiritual courage — maintaining faith, dignity, and defiance in the face of torture and death.

## The Rebbe's Own Words

### Before the Arrest (1920)

When G.P.U. agents entered his synagogue with pistols:
> *"I have already declared that I will not abandon my principles. No man or demon has been born, nor will be born that will make me budge even slightly."*

### At the Arrest (June 14, 1927)

When G.P.U. agents burst into his home at midnight:
> *"I have not feared, I do not fear, and I will not fear them."*

Refusing to sign false document:
> *"To me the entire visit and search is suspect... Do as you will, but Schneersohn will not be arrested with a web of deceit."*

To the armed agents:
> *"Either you are completely in error, or someone has fabricated a libel against us. In any event, it makes no difference to me."*

### In Prison (Spalerno)

Despite conditions where prisoners were taken out to be shot ten minutes after pleading for life:
- **Put on tefillin and prayed daily**
- **Refused to sign false confessions**
- **Maintained composure and dignity**
- **Defied psychological torture**

### At Exile to Kostroma

Parting address at the train station:

> *"We raise our lips in prayer to G-d: 'Yehi Hashem Elokeinu imanu ka'asher haya im avoteinu. Al ya'azveinu ve'al yitsheinu!' — May G-d be with us as He was with our ancestors, to neither forsake nor abandon us."*

> *"However, all the nations of the world must know the following: Only our bodies were sent into exile and subjugated to alien rule; our souls were not given over into captivity and foreign rule."*

> *"No one can impose his belief upon us, nor coerce us to conduct ourselves contrary to our beliefs."*

> *"Imprisonment and hard labor are only of this physical world and of brief duration, while Torah, mitzvot, and the Jewish people are eternal."*

## Reflections Years Later

The Rebbe's words on **12 Tammuz 5705 (1945)**:

> *"I was confined for nineteen days. At such a time one is subject to the ordeal of controlling one's eyes, sealing one's ears, and desisting from speech."*

> *"In that period of my life, I lost all sense of gratification that is derived from material things, not only for a while, but permanently. Then I did not think of myself at all."*

> *"What thoughts could I have about myself while being constantly confronted with the fragility of life? I heard the begging of the prisoners, pleading for life, only to see them taken out to be shot ten minutes later."*

> *"My own idea then was that the initial decay of a seed is a preliminary necessity for later flourishing and growth."*

> *"I never experienced a sense of solitude; I was always mindful of the fact that I possessed revered ancestors: my father, grandfather, great grandfather, and all the luminous, holy figures whose courage and merit would endure eternally."*

## Principles of Spiritual Courage

### 1. Connection to Ancestors

The Rebbe drew strength from his lineage:
- **Father**: Rabbi Shalom Dovber (Rebbe Rashab)
- **Grandfather**: Rabbi Shmuel of Lubavitch (Maharash)
- **Great-grandfather**: Rabbi Menachem Mendel (Tzemach Tzedek)
- **All previous Rebbes** back to Rabbi Shneur Zalman (Alter Rebbe)

He reflected on his father's discourse *"She girds her loins with strength"* (Proverbs 31:17), which he had heard thirty years before.

### 2. Eternal Perspective

> *"Imprisonment and hard labor are only of this physical world and of brief duration, while Torah, mitzvot, and the Jewish people are eternal."*

This perspective enabled:
- Acceptance of pain "with joy"
- Viewing every measure of anguish as increasing fortitude
- Seeing imprisonment as "evoking within us might" for strengthening Judaism

### 3. Public Defiance

The Rebbe refused to remain silent:
- **Publicly condemned** his adversaries
- **Spoke openly** against the "General Assembly" planned by Leningrad's communal leaders
- **Attacked informers** in his Purim 1927 address, frequently mentioning Yevsektzia and adding *"Tzemach shemam"* ("May their names be blotted out!")
- **Maintained his activities** despite knowing he was being watched

### 4. Self-Sacrifice for Others

The **covenant of Adar 5684 (1924)** with nine temimim:
> *"To work to strengthen Judaism with self-sacrifice, 'even to the last drop of blood.'"*

The Rebbe lived this principle, enduring so that:
- **Jewish education** could continue underground
- **Religious observance** could persist
- **Future generations** would have Yiddishkeit

## Examples from Chassidim

### The Rebbe's Daughters

When Nachmanson interrogated them:
- **Shaina**: *"This we are not obligated to explain to you... What we were, we still are; and we declare this openly, regardless of whether you find it acceptable or offensive."*
- **She added**: *"The entire tragedy is that you wish to accomplish everything by power and coercion. This is unethical and repugnant."*

### Reb Michoel Dworkin

- **Went immediately to Kostroma** to prepare for Rebbe's exile
- **Established cheder** under Soviet prohibition
- **Fixed mikveh** for Rebbe's use
- **Danced ecstatically** at liberation: *"Nyet, nyet nikago"* — "Nothing exists aside from G-d!"

### The Nine Temimim

In **Adar 5684 (1924)**, made binding covenant with Rebbe to strengthen Judaism **"even to the last drop of blood."**

## Comparison: Courage vs. Fear

| Those Who Feared | Those With Courage |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Withdrew from communal involvement | Assumed perilous leadership |
| Feared economic privation | Accepted poverty for principle |
| Feared imprisonment and death | Endured both without breaking |
| Abandoned Jewish education | Established underground chadarim |
| Silent in face of persecution | Defiantly spoke truth to power |

## The "Seed" Concept

The Rebbe's insight:
> *"My own idea then was that the initial decay of a seed is a preliminary necessity for later flourishing and growth."*

This expresses:
- **Suffering as planting** — the seed must "decay" (break open) to grow
- **Individual hardship** for communal flourishing
- **The 1927 imprisonment** as catalyst for future Jewish revival
- **Personal loss** of material gratification for spiritual gain
