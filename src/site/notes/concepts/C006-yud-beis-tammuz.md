# Yud-Beis Tammuz (12 Tammuz)

A Chabad holiday celebrating the liberation of the Frierdiker Rebbe (Rabbi Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn) from Soviet imprisonment and exile in 1927.

---

## Historical Background

| Date (5687 / 1927) | Event |
|-------------------|-------|
| 15 Sivan (June 14) | Arrest at 2:15 AM in Leningrad |
| 15 Sivan - 3 Tammuz | Imprisonment (18 days, 11 hours, 15 minutes) |
| 3 Tammuz, 1:30 PM | Capital charge quashed; sentenced to exile in Kostroma |
| 3 Tammuz, 7:30 PM | Train departs for exile |
| 4 Tammuz | Arrives in Kostroma |
| **12 Tammuz (Yud-Beis)** | **Informed of impending release** |
| **13 Tammuz (Yud-Gimmel)** | **Actually liberated at 12:30 PM** |

---

## The Rebbe's Instructions

In a letter written on **15 Sivan 5688 (1928)** — the first anniversary of his arrest — the Rebbe Rayatz urged that these days (Yud-Beis and Yud-Gimmel Tammuz) be set aside as a time of **farbrengen**:

> "A day on which people arouse each other to buttress the Torah and Yiddishkeit in every place according to its needs."

He also blessed:
> "All our brethren who love the Torah and who study and teach it — that G-d open wide His goodly treasure-store and bestow upon them and upon all our fellow Jews life and blessings in endless abundance; that He fortify their hearts with growing resoluteness in the dissemination of Torah and in the upholding of Yiddishkeit."

---

## The Rebbe's Commentary

When publishing the Rebbe Rayatz's account of the imprisonment (*Reshimas HaMaasar*), the Rebbe (Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson) emphasized an important principle in his introduction:

One must balance:
1. **"Buttressing the Torah"** — Making clear that Torah alone defines Yiddishkeit
2. **"In every place according to its needs"** — Sensitive, gradual outreach

> "For this reason, the Rebbe points out, the Rebbe Rayatz stipulates that one's first task is 'to buttress the Torah' — to make it clear to oneself and one's listeners that the only content and defining determinant of Yiddishkeit is the unchangeable Torah given by G-d."

The Rebbe warned: "In one's eagerness to reach out and make Yiddishkeit accessible 'in every place according to its needs,' one might be tempted to dilute or compromise it."

---

## Celebrations

Yud-Beis Tammuz is celebrated with:
- **Farbrengens** (gatherings)
- Study of the liberation story
- Commitments to strengthen Torah and Yiddishkeit
- Reflection on **mesirus nefesh** (self-sacrifice)

The 70th anniversary in 1997 (5757) was marked by the publication of *A Prince in Prison*.
