# Reshimat Hama'aser (Notes of the Arrest)

The Frierdiker Rebbe's autobiographical account of his 1927 arrest, imprisonment, and liberation by the Soviet G.P.U.

## Overview

**Reshimat Hama'aser** (literally "Notes of the Deed" or "Notes of the Arrest") is:
- A **first-person account** written by the Rebbe himself
- Based on notes taken during the imprisonment
- Written on **15 Sivan, 5688** (one year after the arrest)
- A primary source for the events of 1927

## History of the Document

### Original Notes

- The Rebbe **took notes during his imprisonment**
- These notes incorporated observations written in prison
- The original was written by the Rebbe on the first anniversary

### Preservation Story

The preservation of this document is itself a remarkable story:

1. **Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson** (the future Rebbe) painstakingly copied the notes from the Rebbe's journals
2. During WWII, while fleeing the Nazis, the **original was lost**
3. The present work exists only because of Rabbi Menachem Mendel's copy
4. Many years later, when Rabbi Yosef Yitzchak arrived in the U.S., he thanked his son-in-law for saving the historic notes

### The Copy

The copy preserved in Rabbi Menachem Mendel's journals, known as **"Reshimot,"** became the basis for all published versions.

## Content

Reshimat Hama'aser covers:
- The arrest itself (14 Sivan 5687 / June 14, 1927)
- Events leading up to the arrest
- The imprisonment in Spalerno prison
- The interrogation process
- The liberation (12-13 Tammuz 5687 / July 12-13, 1927)

## Publication

Reshimat Hama'aser has been published in:
- *Likkutei Dibburim* (Yiddish Edition), vol. IV, p. 1222 ff.
- *Sefer Hasichot 5687*, pp. 179 IF
- *The Heroic Struggle* (English translation)

## The Rebbe's Purpose

According to the Rebbe's successor (Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson), the Rebbe took these notes:
> *"in order to share his unique chassidic heritage of spiritual faith and courage with others, and thus aid them in bridging the ordeal of spiritual or physical crisis."*

## Characteristics of the Work

The notes exhibit:
- **Perceptive eye for detail** — careful observation of all aspects
- **Remarkable insight into the process of terror** — understanding of psychological tactics
- **Firm courage in the face of brutality** — unwavering faith despite torture
- **Sustained psychological awareness** — understanding of his own reactions and emotions
