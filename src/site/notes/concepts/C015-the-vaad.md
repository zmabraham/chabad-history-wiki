# The Va'ad (1922-1934)

A secret underground organization led by the Frierdiker Rebbe to sustain Jewish religious life in Soviet Russia despite government persecution.

## Overview

The **Va'ad** (Council) was founded in **5682 (1922)** with the Rebbe at its head. It operated secretly to maintain Jewish education, religious practice, and communal life during the period of intense Soviet anti-religious campaigns.

## Activities and Programs

### Legal Advisory Board

- **Organized a jurist legal advisory board**
- Through legal efforts, thwarted many attempts to hinder religious practices
- Successfully restored many synagogues that had been turned into workmen's meeting places

### Cemetery Protection

- **Intervened when Gomel Jewish cemetery was expropriated**
- Stables were being erected on the grounds
- Two hundred coffins had already been removed
- The jurist committee's intervention led Trotsky himself to send a telegram prohibiting such activities

### Religious Education Campaign

- **Mounted intensive petition campaign** throughout the Soviet Union
- Pleaded for permission to provide religious education
- Despite many parents being afraid to have their names known, this effort resulted in a supposed concession:
  - Religious education permitted with significant restriction
  - **"No teacher could have more than three students"**

### Educational Support

- **Funded education of 5,000 children**
- Unfortunately, **"some 16,000 children had to be refused admission for lack of funds"**

### Yeshivot Support

- **Established a small number of yeshivot** that would provide:
  - Room
  - Board
  - Clothing for students
- Set up an **advanced house of study** to train rabbis and shochatim (ritual slaughterers)

### Adult Education

- **Maintained "Tiferet Bachurim"** — night classes for workers and professionals
- Instructed workers and professionals in all matters of Judaism

## The Binding Covenant (1924)

In **Adar of 5681 (1921)**, the Rebbe called a council of many Rabbinical and communal leaders in Moscow to evaluate the situation. After consultations with prominent lawyers, an organization was created to remain and perform its work in Moscow.

In **Adar 5684 (1924)**, at a meeting in Moscow, the Rebbe was elected leader of this organization. He made a **binding "covenant"** with **nine dedicated temimim** (students or ex-students of Tomchei Temimim Lubavitch):

> *"To work to strengthen Judaism with self-sacrifice, 'even to the last drop of blood.'"*

## Post-Arrest Continuation

After the Rebbe's arrest and liberation in 1927, and his eventual departure from Russia, the Va'ad **continued its underground activities** until **5694 (1934)**.

Three members of the Va'ad left Russia and settled in Israel and England. The willingness of members to carry on during this final phase had been conditional upon the Rebbe's acceptance of responsibility for directing the Va'ad from outside Russia.

## Historical Context

The Va'ad operated during:
- **Lenin's and Stalin's rule** — intensifying persecution
- **Yevsektzia's terror** — Jewish Communists actively fighting Judaism
- **Great Purge (1936-1938)** — when even Yevsektzia members were liquidated

## Impact and Legacy

Despite overwhelming odds, the Va'ad:
- **Preserved Jewish education** underground
- **Maintained religious observance** despite prohibition
- **Created networks of resistance** that survived the Soviet period
- **Laid foundation** for post-Soviet Jewish revival
