# First Arrest (1891)

## Overview

The first of seven arrests in the life of Rabbi Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn (the Rayatz), occurring at age 11. This incident established the pattern of fearless defense of Jews that would define his life.

## The Incident

### Date and Location
- **Date:** June 29, 1891 (Yarmarka market day)
- **Location:** Marketplace, Lubavitch
- **Context:** Bustling market fair with farmers, horses, buggies

### What Happened
1. Young Yosef Yitzchak (age 11, almost 12) walked with friend \[\[Shimon Rabinowitz\]\]
2. They met \[\[Reb Dovid the Butcher\]\] carrying livestock
3. A police sergeant brutally attacked Reb Dovid "out of nowhere"
4. Young Yosef Yitzchak defended Reb Dovid
5. The sergeant ordered Yosef Yitzchak's arrest
6. Accused of "ripping off the copper badge" and preventing police duty

### Imprisonment
- **Location:** Volost (local police station)
- **Treatment:** Smacked in face, grabbed by ear, thrown into dark cell
- **Conditions:** Dark cell, doors slammed shut

## Significance

### First of Seven Arrests
This was the first of seven arrests in Yosef Yitzchak's life:
1. **1891:** Age 11, defending Reb Dovid (Lubavitch)
2. **1904:** Saint Petersburg mission
3. **1906:** Saint Petersburg mission
4. **1927:** June 14, midnight arrest (Leningrad)
5. **1927:** During imprisonment
6. **1927:** Additional interrogation
7. **1928:** Brief detention

### Pattern Established
- **Fearless defense:** Standing up to authority to protect Jews
- **Youthful courage:** Only 11 years old
- **Willingness to suffer:** Imprisoned for doing what's right

### Statement from Biography
> "Fearlessly standing up to others would become the motif of the life of Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn."

## Historical Context

### Czarist Russia
- Police brutality against Jews was common
- Jews had limited legal protections
- Markets were tense spaces of potential conflict

### Yarmarka (Market Fair)
- Two-day event: June 29-30, 1891
- Doubled Lubavitch's population (2,000 → 4,000)
- Brought farmers, craftsmen, vendors from surrounding areas
