# Spalerno Prison (Spalerka)

The notorious **G.P.U. prison** in Leningrad where the Frierdiker Rebbe was imprisoned for 19 days in 1927.

## Names

- **Spalerno** — The prison's formal name
- **Spalerka** — Affectionate diminutive used by the Rebbe
- **Tractate Geihinom** — The Rebbe's symbolic name for it ("Talmud of Hell")

## Description

Spalerno was:
- Located in **Leningrad** (now St. Petersburg)
- A **G.P.U. (secret police)** detention facility
- Known for harsh conditions and psychological torture
- A place where many prisoners were taken out to be shot

## The Rebbe's Imprisonment (1927)

### Duration
- **19 days**: 14 Sivan - 3 Tammuz 5687 (June 14 - July 3, 1927)

### Conditions

The Rebbe experienced:
- **Psychological torture** — constant uncertainty about his fate
- **Physical deprivation** — harsh prison conditions
- **Witnessing executions** — hearing prisoners beg for life, then seeing them taken out to be shot ten minutes later
- **Interrogation** — attempts to extract false confessions

### The Rebbe's Response

Despite these conditions, the Rebbe:
- **Put on tefillin and prayed daily**
- **Refused to sign false documents**
- **Maintained his composure and dignity**
- **Defied psychological pressure**

The Rebbe later reflected:
> *"I heard the begging of the prisoners, pleading for life, only to see them taken out to be shot ten minutes later."*

### Shabbat in Spalerno

The Rebbe later proudly declared:
> *"Thank G-d, I spent that Shabbat in Spalerka!"*

This statement, made years later, exemplified his ability to find spiritual meaning even in imprisonment.

## "Tractate Geihinom"

The Rebbe referred to Spalerno as **"Tractate Geihinom"** (Talmud of Hell), drawing a parallel to:
- **Tractate Gitin** — Divorce
- **Tractate Kiddushin** — Marriage
- **Tractate Bava Kama** — First Gate

By calling it "Tractate Geihinom," the Rebbe:
- Used **dark humor** to describe his experience
- Framed his suffering as a **spiritual learning experience**
- Applied a **Talmudic framework** to understand his ordeal
- Maintained his **chassidic perspective** even in prison

## Historical Significance

Spalerno symbolizes:
1. **Soviet persecution of religion** — The regime's attempt to crush Jewish spirit
2. **Jewish resistance** — The Rebbe's refusal to break under pressure
3. **Mesirus nefesh** — Self-sacrifice for Yiddishkeit
4. **Spiritual victory** — Faith triumphing over terror

## Liberation

The Rebbe was released from Spalerno on **3 Tammuz 5687 (July 3, 1927)** and informed he would be exiled to Kostroma for three years. This partial release led to his complete liberation on **12 Tammuz**.
