# Yevsektzia (Yevsektsiya)

**Jewish Sections of the Communist Party** — Soviet organization of Jewish Communists who led particularly vicious campaigns against religious Judaism from 1918 to the 1930s.

## Name and Meaning

- **Russian**: Евсекция (Yevsektsiya)
- **Full name**: Еврейские секции (Jewish Sections)
- **Purpose**: Communist activity "by and for Jews" — but **completely devoid of religious or traditional Jewish content**

## Historical Background

### Formation

- **1918**: Established by **Stalin's People's Commissariat for Nationality Affairs**
- Created alongside **Yevkom** (Commissariat for Jewish National Affairs)
- Goal: Encourage Jewish acceptance of Communism
- First head: **Semyon (Shimon) Dimanshtein** — formerly thoroughly educated in traditional Judaism

### Ideology

**Dimanshtein's 1918 statement:**
> *"There is no salvation for the Jews on any road other than Communism."*

The Yevsektzia believed:
- All religions were "despicable and detrimental to society"
- **Judaism required especially fierce opposition**
- They must be "more ruthless with rabbis than non-Jewish Communists are with priests"

## Anti-Religious Campaign

### Methods and Policies

The Yevsektzia:
1. **Abolished the kehillah** (traditional Jewish community structure) — July 1919 decree signed by **Samuel (Shmuel) Agurskii** and endorsed by Stalin
2. **Declared chadarim and yeshivot illegal**
3. **Confiscated synagogues and schools**
4. **Harassed and arrested** rabbis, mohalim, and shochatim on spurious charges of espionage and Trotskyism
5. **Conducted show trials** of religious holidays, rituals, and schools
6. **Led anti-Shabbat campaigns**
7. **Restricted or prohibited** baking of matzot
8. **Banned ritual slaughter (shechitah)** and circumcision (milah)
9. **Closed mikva'ot** (ritual baths)

### Publications

- **Der Emes** (1918-1930) — Daily Yiddish newspaper
  - Editor: **Moishe Litvakov** (former Hebrew cantor with large collection of Hebrew books on Chassidus)
  - Published harsh anti-religious propaganda
- **Der Apikoires** — Additional anti-religious publication

### Notable Figures

| Figure | Role | Background |
|--------|------|------------|
| **Semyon Dimanshtein** | Head of Yevkom | Traditional Jewish education |
| **Samuel Agurskii** | Deputy commissar | Signed kehillah abolition |
| **Moishe Litvakov** | Editor of Der Emes | Former Hebrew cantor |
| **Maria Frumkina (Esther)** | Yevsektzia leader | Granddaughter of a rabbi, married to a rabbi |

**Maria Frumkina (Esther)** wrote:
> *"The struggle against the Jewish religion within the Jewish community is being waged 'more energetically and stubbornly, above all, more systematically, than in the non-Jewish environment.'"*

She told American visitor Boris Bogen:
> *"Better for the 'Yevseks' to fight Yiddishkeit than to entrust the process to the tender mercies of the Russians... The danger is that the masses may think that Judaism is exempt from anti-religious propaganda, and therefore, it rests with the Jewish Communists to be even more ruthless with rabbis."*

## Persecution of Chabad

The Yevsektzia specifically targeted Chabad-Lubavitch:

### 1927: Pre-Arrest Campaign

**Moishe Litvakov complained to an acquaintance:**
> *"After extensive investigation we have discovered that the Lubavitcher Rebbe was the primary cause of all these activities and that his agents were involved in all of them."*

> *"We are fully knowledgeable of his actions... We will tear him from his roots and have already commenced gathering the necessary evidence."*

**The activities that provoked them:**
- Young men traveling through Georgia delivering inspiring addresses about Jewish faith
- Restoration of mikva'ot in remote areas
- Deposing Yevsektzia councils from villages
- Legal citations of Russian law permitting religious observance

### The 1927 Arrest

Yevsektzia members directly involved:
- **[[E015-nachmanson]]** — Young Jewish G.P.U. official from Nevel, son of chassid
- **[[E019-lulav]]** — Jewish G.P.U. man from Riga family

Both had **Jewish education backgrounds** but became fierce opponents of religious Judaism.

## Linguistic Campaign

The Yevsektzia sought to **de-Hebraize Yiddish**:
- **Removed final forms** of five Hebrew letters from Soviet Yiddish
- **Excluded Hebraic-religious terms** from Yiddish vocabulary
- **Attempted (but failed)** to Latinize the Hebrew-Yiddish alphabet in 1930

### Satirical Haggadah

**Altshuler's "Hagode"** notorious example:
- Equated **Bedikas Chometz** with "expunging the enemies of the revolution"
- Turned **seder order** into "revolutionary order"
- Replaced **Kiddush** with "Kaddish" for the "entire capitalistic order"
- Replaced **Hallel** with the communist "Internationale"

## Irony of History

**Stalin's purges of 1936-1938:**
- Dimanshtein, Agurskii, Litvakov, Frumkina — all **liquidated**
- Their "reward" was not a plaque on the Kremlin, but execution
- **Prophecy fulfilled**: *"Your destroyers and they that made you waste shall go forth from you"* (Isaiah)

**Der Nister (Pinchos Kahanovich)**, Yiddish writer, later lamented:
> *"We have been left with nothing... we have no G-d and we have no Torah. All we have left are the letters of the Yiddish alphabet."*

And even these were almost taken away by Yevsektzia zealots.
