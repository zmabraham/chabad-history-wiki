# Yoshvim (The "Sitters")

A special class of students in Lubavitch during the time of the Tzemach Tzedek — young men entirely occupied with the study and internalization of Chassidus.

---

## Historical Context

During the Tzemach Tzedek's leadership (mid-19th century), Lubavitch housed two distinct groups of students:

| Aspect | Yeshivah Students | Yoshvim (Sitters) |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Focus** | Revealed Torah (Gemara, poskim) | Chassidus and avodah |
| **Age range** | 14-20 years | Varied |
| **Schedule** | Regular shiurim | All-day beis midrash |
| **Maamarim** | Rarely allowed | Full participation |
| **Goal** | Torah scholarship | Chassidic transformation |

---

## The Yeshivah (Founded 5602 / 1842)

### Faculty
- **R. Yisrael Noach** (Tzemach Tzedek's son) — 2 shiurim weekly
- **R. Levi Yitzchak** (Tzemach Tzedek's son-in-law) — 2 shiurim weekly

### Students
- **Lower class**: 25 students, aged 14-17
- **Higher class**: 35-40 students, aged 17-20
- All gifted and assiduous in Gemara and poskim
- Occasionally studied Tanya and Torah Or
- Generally not allowed to hear maamarim except on Yom Tov and Yamim Nora'im

---

## The Yoshvim

### Composition
1. Young men after marriage
2. Young men before marriage
3. Particularly gifted young students
4. Men whose fathers supported them
5. Those who weren't outstanding scholars but wanted to absorb "the ambiance of Lubavitch"

### Purpose
> "Young men entirely occupied with matters of Chassidus"

### Method
From R. Shmuel Horovitch's account [[E017-shmuel-horovitch]]:

> "In the days of the Tzemach Tzedek, at almost all times through the year there were venerable and hoary chassidim visiting Lubavitch. So there were always opportunities to be of service to great men, to observe meanwhile how they davened and how they ate, how they studied and how they conducted themselves."

> "Sometimes this affected young scholars even more than their actual studies."

---

## Service Over Study

Citing the Talmud (Berachos 7b):

> "Being of service to Torah sages is superior [even] to the study of the Torah itself"

R. Shmuel explained:
> "For study has given a student an intellectual grasp of a concept; attendance on elder chassidim has made of him a servant of G-d and a chassid."

---

## The Ambiance of Lubavitch

The yoshvim sought to absorb not just knowledge, but the **entire way of being** of elder chassidim:
- How they davened
- How they ate
- How they studied
- How they conducted themselves

This transmission of **derech** (path/way) was considered superior to intellectual study alone.
