# Igrot Kodesh (Holy Letters)

A collection of the Frierdiker Rebbe's correspondence, serving as a valuable historical source for his activities, leadership, and perspective on events.

## Overview

**Igrot Kodesh** (literally "Holy Letters") is a multi-volume collection containing:
- The Rebbe's letters to individuals and communities
- Responses to halachic questions
- Correspondence about communal affairs
- Letters documenting historical events
- Personal reflections and guidance

## Historical Value

The letters provide:
1. **Contemporary documentation** of events as they unfolded
2. **Personal insight** into the Rebbe's thinking and decisions
3. **Eyewitness testimony** to historical occurrences
4. **Guidance and leadership** distributed across distances

## Notable Content

### The 1927 Arrest and Liberation

Several letters in Igrot Kodesh provide firsthand accounts of:
- The arrest on 14 Sivan 5687
- The imprisonment and interrogation
- The exile to Kostroma
- The liberation on 12-13 Tammuz
- The return to Leningrad

One example cited in S018:
- *Igrot Kodesh, vol. 2, pp. 64* — Letter about the events

### The Letter About the Liberation

A letter written about the liberation describes:
> *"The great clamor in the land, the prayers and supplications through the saying of Psalms day and night in hundreds of cities, and the proclamation of fast days, all these were heard in the loftiest of Heavens, and G-d influenced the hearts of judges to ease their verdict."*
