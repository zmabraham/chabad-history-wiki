# G.P.U. (Gosudarstvennoye Politicheskoye Upravlenie)

**State Political Directorate** — The secret police of the Soviet Union from 1922 to 1934, predecessor to the **NKVD** and later **KGB**.

## Role in 1927 Arrest

The G.P.U. was the agency that:
- Arrested the Frierdiker Rebbe on **14 Sivan 5687 (June 14, 1927)**
- Imprisoned him for 19 days in **Spalerno Prison** in Leningrad
- Interrogated him and attempted to break his spirit
- Exiled him to **Kostroma** for three years
- Finally granted his complete liberation on **12 Tammuz (July 12)**

## G.P.U. Methods

### At the Rebbe's Home

**Arrest procedure:**
- Burst into dining room shouting after midnight
- "About twenty minutes had passed when the doorbell rang forcefully"
- Armed men stood in line awaiting commands
- Spokesman claimed: *"This is my normal manner of speech. It seems that you are not familiar with the methods of G.P.U. representatives."*

**Threats used:**
- *"The G.P.U. can force even the silent tongue to speak... To them all is revealed, willingly or otherwise."*
- *"I will speak with this and silence your elegant words"* — waving revolver
- Raised revolver at Rebbe's daughter: *"If you speak one more word, then you, too, will be arrested."*

### In Spalerno Prison

- **Psychological torture** — constant uncertainty
- **Physical deprivation** — harsh conditions
- **Witnessing executions** — prisoners taken out to be shot
- **Interrogation tactics** — attempting to extract false confessions

## G.P.U. Structure

The G.P.U. had multiple divisions:
- **Cheka Secret Police** — arrests and interrogations
- **Civil Police** — general law enforcement
- **Civil Investigation Department** — case preparation

## Historical Context

### Evolution of Soviet Secret Police

| Organization | Years | Notes |
|--------------|-------|-------|
| **Cheka** | 1917-1922 | Original Bolshevik secret police |
| **G.P.U.** | 1922-1934 | When Rebbe was arrested |
| **NKVD** | 1934-1946 | Became People's Commissariat |
| **KGB** | 1954-1991 | Committee for State Security |

### Yevsektzia Collaboration

The G.P.U. worked closely with the **Yevsektzia** (Jewish Sections of Communist Party):
- Jewish Communists provided intelligence
- **Moishe Litvakov** (editor of *Der Emes*) boasted: *"The G.P.U. is aware of Rabbi Schneersohn's crime, and we have decided to uproot him."*
- Yevsektzia members like **[[E015-nachmanson]]** and **[[E019-lulav]]** were G.P.U. agents

## 1927 Context

The arrest occurred during:
- **Wave of executions** across Russia
- **Foreign diplomat assassinations**
- **Bombings** and terrorist acts
- **Paranoid atmosphere** — government saw enemies everywhere

**New York Times headlines:**
- *"Executions Spread Terror in Russia, Europe is Shocked"*
- *"Moscow in Panic Under Mailed Fist; Foreigners Leaving"*

## Rebbe's Response to G.P.U.

Despite G.P.U. tactics, the Rebbe maintained:
- **Dignity**: *"I have not feared, I do not fear, and I will not fear them."*
- **Defiance**: Refused to sign documents falsely claiming legal arrest
- **Principle**: *"No one can impose his belief upon us, nor coerce us to conduct ourselves contrary to our beliefs."*
- **Faith**: Continued to pray with tefillin daily in prison

## Liberation Factors

The Rebbe's release resulted from:
1. **International pressure** — world outcry at arrest
2. **Diplomatic intervention** — Western governments protested
3. **Petitions and prayers** — Jews worldwide campaigned
4. **Madam Peshkova's efforts** — Political Prisoner Relief organization
5. **High-level Soviet decision** — Order came from Moscow

## G.P.U. Files

The Rebbe's G.P.U. file was **declassified** and included as:
- **Appendix 8** in *The Heroic Struggle*
- Provides Soviet perspective on the case
- Shows charges of "counter-revolutionary activity"
- Reveals the extent of surveillance
