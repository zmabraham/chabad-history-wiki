# Undaunted - Chapter 2: Tomchei Temimim

## Overview

Chapter 2 covers the founding and early development of Yeshivas Tomchei Temimim, the revolutionary educational institution that integrated Talmud and Chasidic philosophy. The yeshiva was founded as the Rayatz's wedding gift from his father, marking the beginning of his leadership in Jewish education.

## Key Events

### Engagement (1896)
- **Date:** June 9, 1896 (28 Sivan, 5646)
- **Location:** Bolivke (summer resort)
- **Event:** Rayatz engaged to second cousin Nechama Dina Schneersohn
- **Celebration:** Lasted a week
- **Significance:** Marriage connection chosen over wealthier prospects

### Wedding (1897)
- **Date:** September 10, 1897 (13 Elul, 5657)
- **Location:** Lubavitch
- **Ceremony:** Friday afternoon in courtyard of main synagogue
- **Details:** Rayatz donned shtreimel for first time
- **Celebration:** Continued Sunday evening in the zal
- **Significance:** Led directly to founding of Tomchei Temilim

### Founding of Tomchei Temimim (1897)
- **Date:** September 12, 1897 (15 Elul, 5657)
- **Occasion:** Wedding reception meeting
- **Participants:** 50 distinguished wedding guests
- **Catalyst:** Rayatz's wedding gift request to his father
- **Revolution:** Integration of Talmud and chasidic philosophy
- **Significance:** First systematic chasidic yeshivah curriculum

### First Day of Studies (1897)
- **Date:** September 15, 1897 (18 Elul)
- **Significance:** Birthday of Baal Shem Tov and Alter Rebbe
- **First mashpia:** Rabbi Shmuel Gronem
- **Students:** Original group of 18

### Rayatz Appointed Director (1898)
- **Date:** September 11, 1898 (24 Elul, 5659)
- **Age:** 18 years old
- **Initiative:** Group of Chasidim led by Rabbi Asher Grossman
- **Process:** appealed to Rashab when Rayatz demurred
- **Significance:** Beginning of Rayatz's leadership in education

### Mission Statement (1900)
- **Date:** Simchat Torah 5661
- **Metaphor:** Students as soldiers of King David
- **Mission:** Disseminate chasidic teachings with self-sacrifice
- **Requirement:** Divorce from worldly concerns

### Hebron Branch (1911)
- **Decision:** 1910 to open branch in Eretz Yisrael
- **Property:** Beit Romano purchased (1908)
- **Team:** Led by Rabbi Zalman Havlin
- **Significance:** First international expansion; first chasidic yeshivah in Holy Land

## Revolutionary Concept

### Educational Integration
Tomchei Temilim was revolutionary because it:
- **Integrated** Talmud study with chasidic philosophy
- **Systematized** chasidic teachings in a curriculum
- **Created** a new model for Jewish education
- **Produced** leaders who could disseminate chasidic teachings

### Student Mission
The Rashab defined students as:
- **Soldiers of King David**
- **Self-sacrificing** for the mission
- **Divorced** from worldly concerns
- **Dedicated** to disseminating chasidic philosophy
