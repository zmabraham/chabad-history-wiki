# Toldot Chabad bi-Artzot HaBrit (History of Chabad in the United States)

## Overview

**תולדות חב"ד בארצות הברית** (History of Chabad in the United States) is a comprehensive historical work documenting the establishment and development of the Chabad-Lubavitch movement in America.

## Languages

- **Primary:** Yiddish (יידיש)
- **Secondary:** Hebrew (עברית) sections
- **Total lines:** 41,661

## Key Topics Covered

### Early American Period (1920s-1940s)
- Arrival of Chabad emissaries in America
- Establishment of early communities
- Correspondence with European leadership

### The Rebbe Rayatz in America (1940-1950)
- Arrival in March 1940 (9 Adar II)
- Establishment of headquarters in Brooklyn (770 Eastern Parkway)
- Founding of American institutions

### Institutional Development
- **Tomchei Temimim Yeshiva** - American yeshiva network
- **Kehot Publication Society (Otzar HaChassidim)** - Publishing house
- **Merkos L'Inyonei Chinuch** - Educational organizations
- **Community organizations** across multiple cities

### Community Development
- Correspondence with community members
- Reports on activities and growth
- Leadership directives and guidance
- Development of Chabad communities across US cities

## Historical Significance

This period (1940s onward) marked:
- **Transfer of leadership** from Europe to America
- **Establishment of global Chabad movement** from Brooklyn
- **Founding of institutions** that would enable worldwide outreach
- **Preservation of Torah scholarship** after Holocaust devastation
