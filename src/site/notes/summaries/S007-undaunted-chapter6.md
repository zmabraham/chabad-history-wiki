# Undaunted - Chapter 6: The Underground Years

## Overview

Chapter 6 covers the period of underground Jewish educational activities under Soviet persecution — the secret network of schools, teachers, and students who maintained Torah learning despite government prohibition.

## Key Themes

### Underground Education Network
- Secret schools across Soviet Union
- Hidden curriculum of Torah and Chasidic teachings
- Teachers and students risked arrest
- Thousands of children participated

### Soviet Persecution
- Government ban on religious education
- GPU surveillance of Jewish communities
- Arrests of educators
- Closing of synagogues and religious institutions

### Tomchei Temilim Underground
- Yeshiva continued operating in secret
- Students disguised as secular students
- Maamarim taught in private homes
- Network extended across multiple cities

## Key Activities

- **Secret teaching:** Torah study hidden from authorities
- **Mock summer camps:** Disguised religious education
- **Correspondence courses:** Remote learning system
- **Teacher training:** Preparing new generation of educators

## Historical Significance

This period demonstrated:
- Resilience of Jewish education under persecution
- Creative adaptation to Soviet constraints
- Commitment to Torah transmission
- Foundation for post-war Chabad revival
