# Undaunted - Chapter 9: The World is Shattered

## Overview

Chapter 9 covers the outbreak of World War II in September 1939 and the miraculous rescue operation that saved the Rebbe from Nazi-occupied Poland — a dramatic story involving diplomats, intelligence agents, and Chabad activists across multiple countries.

## Key Events

### Final Meeting Before War (August 26, 1939)
- **Location:** Otwock, Poland
- **Participants:** Rebbe and Rabbi Yisrael Jacobson
- **Topic:** Rebbe's planned relocation to US
- **Rebbe's words:** "In about half a year, with G-d's assistance, we will come to America"
- **Five days later:** Germany invaded Poland (September 1, 1939)

### War Breaks Out (September 1, 1939)
- **4:45 AM:** German battleship shelled Gdańsk
- **Morning:** Panzer tanks invaded Poland
- **8:00 AM:** German planes bombed Otwock
- **Context:** Yeshivah students celebrating founding farbrengen at dawn

### Twelve-Week Ordeal
The Rebbe endured twelve weeks under Nazi rule in Warsaw — suffering he later said exceeded even his Soviet imprisonment.

### The Rescue Operation
A multinational effort involving:
- **Brooklyn:** Chabad activists launching campaign
- **Washington:** Government officials pressured
- **Riga:** Chasidim coordinating escape
- **Berlin:** Diplomats negotiating
- **Abwehr:** German intelligence officers helping
- **Wehrmacht:** Soldiers of Jewish background assisting

### Escape from Warsaw
- **Rosh Hashanah:** Bombing forced evacuation to medical clinic
- **Danger:** Nazi regime actively hunting Rebbe
- **American intervention:** US officials pressured for release
- **German helpers:** Abwehr agents including Bloch escorted Rebbe

### Journey to Freedom
- **Escape route:** Warsaw → Nazi Germany → Latvian border → Riga
- **Arrival in Riga:** December 17, 1939 (5 Tevet 5700)
- **Mood:** "We were very, very happy" to escape Nazi regime

## Key People

| Person | Role | Significance |
|--------|------|--------------|
| Rabbi Yisrael Jacobson | US Chabad leader | Last meeting before war; launched rescue campaign |
| Rabbi Yechezkel Feigin | Secretary | Documented the ordeal; managed precious manuscripts |
| Colonel Bloch | Abwehr officer | Escorted Rebbe across Nazi Germany; died 1945 |
| Wilhelm Canaris | Abwehr chief | Approved rescue; hanged 1945 for anti-Hitler plot |
| Rabbi Shmuel Levitin | US leader | Coordinated rescue efforts |

## Historical Significance

This rescue demonstrated:
- International Chabad network effectiveness
- Divine providence in survival
- Complexity of wartime escape
- Foundation for future American leadership
