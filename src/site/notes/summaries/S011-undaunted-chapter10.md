# Undaunted - Chapter 10: America Iz Nisht Aderesh

## Overview

Chapter 10 covers the Rebbe's arrival in America on March 19, 1940 (9 Adar II) — the beginning of a new chapter that would transform American Judaism. The title means "America is no different" — the Rebbe's declaration that uncompromising Torah Judaism could thrive in the New World.

## Key Events

### Voyage to America
- **Departure:** Riga via Gothenburg, Sweden
- **Ship:** Swedish liner Drottningholm
- **Journey:** Twelve days across North Atlantic
- **Hazards:** Stopped by British warships and German submarines
- **Arrival:** Monday, March 18, 4:00 PM in New York Harbor
- **Disembarkation:** Tuesday, March 19, 1940 (9 Adar II) at Pier 97, West 57th Street

### First Words on American Soil
Upon being welcomed by US Chabad leadership, the Rebbe immediately turned to practical matters:

> "The suffering I endured in prison in Russia does not compare to the torments of the twelve weeks I spent under their [Nazi] rule. Now we will quickly take care of the formalities and get to work immediately. Our work is Torah and Yiddishkeit."

He quoted from morning prayers: "Hashem is hanoten laya'eif koach — the One who gives strength to those who are weary" and "al kol neshimah veneshimah tehallel Kah — we must give thanks and praise to G-d for every breath."

### Welcome at the Pier
- **Crowd:** Thousands of Jews of all types
- **Press:** Der Tog wrote the pier could have hosted Agudas Harabonim convention
- **Diversity:** "Young and old, rich and poor, bearing the traditional kaftan, black hats, beards and peyos, or the most elaborate creations of Parisian courtiers"

### Impact on Arriving Youth
- **Yitzchok Groner** (age 16): Sent by Rebbe to Australia in 1947, led Chabad there until 2008
- **Risya Kazarnovsky** (age 13): Became pioneering emissary with husband Rabbi Zalman Posner in Nashville, 1949

## The Mission

### Rebbe's Vision for America
The ten-day voyage from Riga to New York was the Rebbe's "Ten Days of Penitence and decision-making about the kind of life that I would have to undertake."

His mission: "The task of bringing to America the mesirat nefesh (self-sacrifice) for unadulterated Torah study that my revered father implanted in Tomchei Temimim."

**Goal:** "America should replace Europe as a center of Torah."

As the Young Israel Viewpoint wrote: "With his arrival the center of gravity of Jewish life shifts from the old world to the new."

### Previous American Experience
The 1929-30 visit had prepared the way:
- Understanding of American Jewish landscape
- Established support network
- Knowledge of "lay of the land"
- Broad vision for impact

## Historical Significance

The Rebbe's arrival marked:
- Transfer of Chabad leadership to America
- Beginning of transformation of American Torah Judaism
- Foundation for global Jewish outreach movement
- New era of post-war Jewish revitalization

## Key Theme

**"America Iz Nisht Aderesh"** — The Rebbe's declaration that America was spiritually no different from Europe; authentic Torah Judaism could and must thrive in the New World.
