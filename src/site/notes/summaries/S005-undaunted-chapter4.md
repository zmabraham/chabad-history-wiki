# Undaunted - Chapter 4: Armed Men at Midnight

## Overview

Chapter 4 covers the infamous 1927 arrest of the Rebbe Rayatz by the GPU (Soviet secret police) — the midnight arrest that began a 19-day ordeal of imprisonment, interrogation, and ultimate liberation. This event became a defining moment in Chabad history.

## The Arrest

### Date and Time
- **Date:** June 15, 1927 (15 Sivan 5687)
- **Time:** Between midnight and 1:00 AM
- **Location:** 22 Mochovaya Street, Leningrad
- **Perpetrators:** Armed GPU agents (Soviet secret police)

### Family Present
- Rebbetzin Nechama Dina
- Daughters: Chaya Mushka, Sheina, Chana, Mushka (Moussia)
- Son-in-law: Rashag (Rabbi Shmaryahu Gourary)

## The 19-Day Ordeal

### Initial Detention
- Transported to Shpalerka prison
- Interrogations with death threats
- Tefillin confiscated (later returned after hunger strike)

### International Pressure
- World Jewish community mobilized
- International protests and diplomatic pressure
- Appeals to Soviet authorities

### Sentence and Commutation
- Initial sentence: Death penalty
- Commuted to: Exile in Solovki
- Final sentence: 3 years exile in Kostroma

### Liberation
- **Date:** July 3, 1927 (3 Tamuz)
- **Duration:** 19 days in captivity
- **Result:** Complete liberation on 12 Tamuz

## Historical Significance

This arrest was:
- The most severe of seven arrests
- A defining moment in Soviet-Jewish relations
- Demonstration of fearless resistance to communism
- Catalyst for international Jewish solidarity
- Foundation for annual 12-13 Tamuz commemorations
