# Undaunted - Chapter 7: The Voyage

## Overview

Chapter 7 covers the Rebbe's yearlong journey from 1929-1930 — travels to America and Eretz Yisrael to secure funding for Russian Jews and visit the graves of righteous predecessors.

## Key Events

### Decision to Travel (1929)
- **Crisis:** JDC suspended funding for Rebbe's work in Russia
- **JDC shift:** Partnered with Communist regime instead
- **Rebbe's response:** "The disheartening news regarding the JDC budget compels me to travel successfully to the US"
- **Consultation:** Sought guidance from Rabbi Chaim Ozer and the Chafetz Chaim

### Journey to America
- **Route:** Riga → Eretz Yisrael → America
- **Purpose:** Two dilemmas - help Russian Jews, visit graves of tzadikim
- **Arrival:** March 1929 in New York Harbor

### Eretz Yisrael Visit
- Visited graves of righteous ancestors
- Spiritual rejuvenation
- Connection to holy sites

### American Visit (1929-1930)
- Met with JDC leadership in New York
- Fundraising for Russian Jewish communities
- Established American support network
- Strengthened traditional Judaism in US

## Key People

| Person | Role | Significance |
|--------|------|--------------|
| Yehuda Lokshin | Chabad director in NY | Recipient of first indication of travel plans |
| Rabbi Chaim Ozer | Torah leader | Encouraged the trip |
| Chafetz Chaim | Torah sage | Encouraged the trip |

## Historical Context

- **1927:** Rebbe's arrest and exile created crisis for Russian Jews
- **1928:** JDC shifted support to Communist regime
- **1929:** Lunacharsky declared "We shall combat the Schneersohnovschina"
- **Great Depression:** Emerging economic crisis limited fundraising potential

## Outcomes

The journey established:
- American Chabad support network
- Direct funding channels for Russian Jews
- Foundation for future American relationship
- Preparation for eventual 1940 immigration
