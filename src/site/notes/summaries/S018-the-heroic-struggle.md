# The Heroic Struggle: The Arrest and Liberation of Rabbi Yosef Y. Schneersohn of Lubavitch

**Source:** Kehot Publication Society, 1999. Translated and adapted from the memoirs of the Rebbe by Rabbi Dr. Alter B. Z. Metzger.

## Overview

This comprehensive work documents the 1927 arrest, imprisonment, and liberation of the Frierdiker Rebbe, Rabbi Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn, by the Soviet G.P.U. (secret police). The book is a primary source compilation that includes:

- **Reshimat Hama'aser** (Notes of the Arrest) — the Rebbe's autobiographical account written on 15 Sivan 5688, one year after his arrest
- **Yagdil Torah** journal account of the prison cell experiences
- **Di Yisurim fun Lubavitcher Rebben in Soviet Russland** (Riga 1930) — covering the first interrogation through liberation
- Recently declassified G.P.U. files
- Eyewitness accounts from chassidim including Reb Eliyahu Chaim Althaus
- Historical background on Soviet anti-religious campaigns

## Historical Context

### Soviet War on Yiddishkeit

The book provides extensive background on the systematic Communist campaign to eliminate Jewish religious observance:

- **1917**: Bolshevik Revolution promises "abolition of national and religious disabilities"
- **1918**: Creation of **Yevkom** (Commissariat for Jewish National Affairs) and **Yevsektzia** (Jewish Sections of Communist Party)
- **1919**: Kehillah (Jewish communal structure) abolished
- **1921**: First anti-Jewish trials begin; cheder of Vitebsk and Lubavitcher yeshivah in Rostov pronounced illegal
- **1922**: Sunday established as day of rest
- **1929**: Decree mandates no cessation of work and no common day of rest

Key figures in the anti-religious campaign:
- **Semyon (Shimon) Dimanshtein** — head of Yevkom, formerly traditionally educated
- **Samuel (Shmuel) Agurskii** — deputy commissar who signed kehillah abolition decree
- **Moishe Litvakov** — former Hebrew cantor, editor of Der Emes, possessed large collection of Hebrew books on Chassidus
- **Maria Yakovleva Frumkina (Esther)** — leader in Yevsektzia, granddaughter of a rabbi, author of "Down with the Rabbis"

All were later liquidated during Stalin's purges of 1936-1938.

### The Va'ad Organization

Founded in 1922 with the Rebbe at its head, this underground organization:

1. Organized jurist legal advisory board that restored many synagogues
2. Intervened when Gomel Jewish cemetery was expropriated (Trotsky personally telegrammed to stop it)
3. Mounted intensive petition campaign for religious education
4. Funded education of 5,000 children (16,000 had to be refused for lack of funds)
5. Established small number of yeshivot providing room, board, and clothing
6. Set up advanced house of study to train rabbis and shochatim
7. Maintained "Tiferet Bachurim" night classes for workers and professionals

## Chronology of Events

### Prelude to Arrest (1920-1927)

- **Tammuz 5680 (1920)**: Yevsektzia agents enter Rebbe's synagogue with pistols drawn; Rebbe declares: "I have already declared that I will not abandon my principles. No man or demon has been born, nor will be born that will make me budge even slightly."
- **Iyar 5681 (1921)**: Libelous campaign produces decree to close Rebbe's yeshivah; all property confiscated
- **Nissan 5684 (1924)**: Rebbe returns to Rostov, visited by soldiers proposing voluntary imprisonment; after negotiations, allowed to leave for Leningrad
- **Adar 5684 (1924)**: Rebbe elected leader of Moscow organization; makes binding "covenant" with nine temimim to work "even to the last drop of blood"
- **5685-5686 (1925-1926)**: Terror weakens somewhat; Rebbe establishes yeshivot to teach Chassidus
- **Rosh Hashanah 5687 (1927)**: Number of visitors exceeds capacity of Rebbe's home
- **June 1927**: Wave of executions and terror across Russia; foreign diplomats assassinated

### The Arrest

**Date:** Tuesday night, 14 Sivan 5687 (June 14, 1927), shortly after midnight

The arrest came after the Rebbe had concluded receiving people for private audiences (which ran from 7 PM to 11:30 PM). Key G.P.U. officials present:

- **Nachmanson** — young Jewish G.P.U. official from Nevel, son of a chassid who had traveled to Lubavitch
- **Lulav** — short dark-haired Jewish man from Riga family

The Rebbe's response to the armed G.P.U. agents: *"I have not feared, I do not fear, and I will not fear them."*

His daughters Chaya Moussia and Shaina defiantly told Nachmanson they were "members of our father's party" and would not be intimidated.

### Imprisonment at Spalerno

**Duration:** 19 days (14 Sivan - 3 Tammuz)

The notorious Spalerno prison in Leningrad was the site of:

- Harsh interrogations
- Psychological torture
- witnessing prisoners taken out to be shot
- The Rebbe's unwavering faith and dignity

Despite conditions, the Rebbe:
- Put on tefillin and prayed daily
- Maintained his composure and principles
- Refused to sign false documents

### Exile to Kostroma

**3 Tammuz (July 3)**: After 19 days in Spalerno, told he could return home for 6 hours, then must take train to Kostroma for 3-year exile.

The Rebbe's parting words at the train station:
> *"Only our bodies were sent into exile and subjugated to alien rule; our souls were not given over into captivity and foreign rule... No one can impose his belief upon us, nor coerce us to conduct ourselves contrary to our beliefs."*

In Kostroma:
- Rebbe arrived late night, 4 Tammuz
- Reported to G.P.U. next morning; told he was "an exile-prisoner" and "criminal receiving punishment"
- Rebbe Michoel Dworkin had already arrived, established a cheder and fixed the mikveh
- The shochet of Kostroma provided lodging

### The Liberation

**12 Tammuz (July 12)**: The Rebbe's 47th birthday — the day of liberation

The local G.P.U. official informed him: *"You are totally freed from the need of any further appearances. The order has been received to grant you full freedom."*

Upon hearing the news, Rebbe Michoel Dworkin danced around the house singing "Nyet, nyet nikago" — "Nothing exists aside from G-d!"

The Rebbe delivered the ma'amar "G-d is among those that help me" that day.

**13 Tammuz**: Received Certificate of Release; delivered ma'amar "Baruch HaGomel" (Blessed be the One Who bestows good upon the unworthy)

**14 Tammuz**: Left Kostroma as free citizen

**15 Tammuz**: Arrived home in Leningrad; called to Torah (Parshat Pinchas) and recited HaGomel blessing

### Departure from Russia

Following continued threats from Lulav, Nachmanson, and Messing, the Rebbe moved to Malachovka (near Moscow) and eventually left Russia entirely — with the help of international diplomatic pressure including:
- **Dr. Hildesheimer and Dr. Leo Baeck** of Berlin
- **Madam Peshkova** of the Political Prisoner Relief organization
- The Frankfurt Jewish community's invitation

## Key Themes

### Mesirus Nefesh (Self-Sacrifice)

The Rebbe's unwavering commitment to Yiddishkeit despite:
- Threats of imprisonment and death
- Physical and psychological torture
- Pressure to abandon principles
- Witnessing executions of fellow prisoners

### Faith Under Duress

The Rebbe maintained:
- Daily prayer with tefillin
- Refusal to sign false documents
- Dignified defiance in face of terror
- Spiritual leadership even from prison

### International Solidarity

The liberation resulted from:
- World outcry at the arrest
- Diplomatic pressure from abroad
- Petitions and prayers worldwide
- Media coverage in Western press

### Spiritual Impact

The Rebbe reflected years later:
> *"I lost all sense of gratification that is derived from material things, not only for a while, but permanently. Then I did not think of myself at all... I never experienced a sense of solitude; I was always mindful of the fact that I possessed revered ancestors."*
