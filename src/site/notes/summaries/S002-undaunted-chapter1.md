# Undaunted - Chapter 1: Lubavitch

## Overview

Chapter 1 covers the early life of young Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn in Lubavitch, culminating in his first arrest at age 11 for defending a Jew from police brutality. This incident established the pattern of fearless defense that would define his life.

## Key Events

### 1891: First Arrest (Age 11)
- **Date:** June 29, 1891 (Yarmarka market day)
- **Location:** Marketplace, Lubavitch
- **Incident:** Young Yosef Yitzchak defends Reb Dovid the butcher from police sergeant brutality
- **Outcome:** Arrested, imprisoned in volost station
- **Significance:** First of seven arrests; demonstrated fearless defense of Jews

### Context: Lubavitch as Chabad Capital
- **1813:** Rabbi Dovber (Mitteler Rebbe) settles in Lubavitch
- **102 years:** Lubavitch serves as Chabad capital (1813-1915)
- **Population:** ~2,000 residents, doubled during market fairs

## Birth and Early Life

### Birth (1880)
- **Date:** June 30, 1880 (12 Tamuz 5640)
- **Location:** Lubavitch, White Russia
- **Parents:** Rabbi Shalom Dovber (Rashab) and Rebbetzin Shterna Sara

### The Prophetic Dream (1879)
Rebbetzin Shterna Sara's dream on Simchat Torah 1879:
- Overlooked for blessing by Rabbi Shmuel (Maharash)
- Dreams of three Jews who promise her a son
- Identified as first three Chabad Rebbes (Alter Rebbe, Mitteler Rebbe, Tzemach Tzedek)
- Prophetic fulfillment: Yosef Yitzchak born the following year

### Bar Mitzvah (1893)
- **Date:** Monday, June 26, 1893 (Tamuz 26)
- **Location:** Lubavitch
- **Activities:**
  - 6:00 AM: Mikveh immersion
  - Prayed at gravesites of Rabbi Menachem Mendel and Rabbi Shmuel
  - Weeklong festivities, maamarim, blessings

## Leadership Transitions Covered

### Maharash's Passing (1882)
- **Date:** Shortly after Yom Kippur 1882
- **Age:** 48
- **Last words:** "You will rise up thirty-two steps in a physical dimension and I will rise up thirty-two steps in a spiritual way"
- **Leadership crisis:** Two sons (Raza and Rashab) initially refuse leadership

### Rashab Accepts Leadership (1893)
- **Date:** Rosh HaShanah 1893
- **Age:** 32
- **Significance:** After 11 years of hesitation, fully assumes role
- **Era:** "Era of rebirth of Lubavitch"

### Rashab Becomes Rebbe (1920)
- Yosef Yitzchak succeeds his father at age 40
- Becomes sixth Lubavitcher Rebbe

## Lubavitch: Historical Significance

### Timeline
- **1813:** Rabbi Dovber settles in Lubavitch after Napoleon's invasion
- **1915, October 24 (17 Cheshvan):** Rashab and Rayatz flee German invasion
- **1936:** Last synagogue closed by communist authorities
- **1941, July 21:** Nazis seize Lubavitch
- **1941, November 4:** 483 Jews murdered; ghetto destroyed

## Key Themes

1. **Fearless Defense** — Standing up against oppression begins at age 11
2. **Lubavitch as Home** — Spiritual and physical center of Chabad
3. **Leadership Crisis** — Pattern of reluctant leadership acceptance
4. **Divine Providence** — Prophetic dreams and fulfilled blessings
