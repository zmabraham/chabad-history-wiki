# Undaunted - Chapter 3: The Secret Covenant

## Overview

Chapter 3 covers the transition of leadership from the Rashab to the Rayatz in 1920, including the private "secret covenant" between father and son that would define the Rayatz's approach to leadership.

## Key Events

### Rashab's Passing and Burial (1920)
- **Date:** Late Saturday night (passing), Sunday afternoon (funeral/burial)
- **Location:** Rostov
- **Attendance:** All local Jews, yeshivah students, elders
- **Rayatz's response:** Led evening prayers in tears
- **Significance:** End of an era; leadership transition begins

### The Secret Covenant
After the burial, the Rayatz confided in Berel Rivkin about his father's warnings he hadn't comprehended. The Rashab had prepared his son for leadership through private instructions, creating a "secret covenant" between them.

### First Maamar (End of Shivah)
- **Timing:** Shabbat afternoon, end of week of mourning
- **Location:** Rashab's private study in Rostov
- **Participant:** Berel Rivkin (private delivery)
- **Significance:** First step toward accepting leadership

### Passover Maamarim (1920)
- **First day of Passover:** Private maamar to Rivkin, others listened from adjoining room
- **End of Passover:** Maamar with Chasidim present
- **Description:** "Exactly like when the Rebbe Rashab said a maamar"
- **Significance:** Public acceptance of leadership role

### Yeshivah Relocation (Winter 1920)
- **From:** Kremenchuk
- **To:** Rostov
- **Reason:** Fear of starvation
- **Significance:** Yeshivah under Rayatz's direct supervision

### Typhus Illness (November 1920)
- **Date:** November 8, 1920 (28 Marcheshvan 5681)
- **Context:** Typhus epidemic in Rostov
- **Severity:** Near-fatal; unable to lead services
- **Recovery:** Had strength to say maamar on following Shabbat
- **Significance:** Health challenge during early leadership

## Key People

| Person | Role | Significance |
|--------|------|--------------|
| \[\[Berel Rivkin\]\] | Confidant | Recipient of first maamarim; witness to transition |

## The Secret Covenant

The Rashab had prepared his son for leadership through:
1. **Private warnings** about passing that son didn't initially comprehend
2. **Private instructions** to deliver maamarim
3. **Father-son bond** that defined the transition

The Rayatz fulfilled these instructions by:
- Delivering maamarim privately first
- Gradually expanding to public delivery
- Maintaining the covenant's privacy
