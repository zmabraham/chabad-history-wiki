# Toldot Chabad bi-Polin, Lita u-Latvia (Volume 5)

## Overview

**תולדות חב"ד בפולין, ליטא ולטביא** (History of Chabad in Poland, Lithuania, and Latvia) is Volume 5 of the comprehensive Toldot Chabad series. This scholarly work by **שלום דובער לוין** (Shalom Dovber Levine) documents Chabad history in these regions, primarily covering the interwar period (1920-1945) and including earlier history from the Alter Rebbe's time.

**Written:** 11 Nisan 5771 (April 2011)
**Published by:** ספריית ליובאוויטש (Lubavitch Library)

## Primary Focus

### Tomchei Tmimim Yeshiva Network
The book's main focus is the **Tomchei Tmimim** yeshiva network:
- **Warsaw (1921)** - Beginning of the network
- **Vilna (1941)** - Pre-war expansion
- **Radom Ghetto (1942)** - Tragic continuation during Holocaust
- **Kobe, Japan** - Exile community
- **Shanghai, China** - Refugee yeshiva

## Geographic Sections

### 1. חב"ד בליטא (Chabad in Lithuania)
- Historical Chabad communities in Lithuania
- Chabad synagogues and institutions
- Activity during the Rebbe Rayatz period

### 2. חב"ד בלטביא (Chabad in Latvia)
- Settlement of Chabad chassidim in Latvia
- Activity during the Riga period (1927-1934)
- Communities and correspondences

### 3. חב"ד בפולין (Chabad in Poland)
- Warsaw as major center
- Otwock yeshiva
- Pre-war community development

## Historical Context

**Period Covered:** 1920-1945 (interwar + WWII)

This was the period when Chabad's center was located in these countries (1928-1940) following the Bolshevik crackdown in Russia. The book documents:

- Chabad emissaries (shluchim) and mashpi'im (influencers)
- Lists of yeshiva students and staff
- Memorial records of those who perished in the Holocaust
- Archival correspondence and documents
- Photographs and primary source material

## Key Events

| Year | Event | Location |
|------|-------|----------|
| 1921 | Tomchei Tmimim Warsaw established | Warsaw |
| 1927 | Rebbe Rayatz moves to Riga | Riga, Latvia |
| 1930s | Expansion throughout Poland/Lithuania | Multiple cities |
| 1939 | Nazi invasion begins | Poland |
| 1940-41 | Escape routes through Japan/China | Far East |
| 1941-45 | Holocaust devastation | Eastern Europe |

## Archival Value

This volume serves as:
- **Memorial** to Holocaust victims from Chabad communities
- **Historical record** of interwar Chabad activities
- **Primary source** collection with letters and documents
- **Genealogical resource** with extensive name lists
